## 1. PROPRIETARY INFORMATION

### 1.1 Definition
"Proprietary Information" means any and all confidential and proprietary information of the Company, whether written, oral, electronic, or visual, including but not limited to:

(a) **Technical Information:** source code, object code, algorithms, AI/ML models and architectures, model weights and parameters, training data and datasets, data processing pipelines, software designs, system architectures, APIs, protocols, formulas, inventions, discoveries, improvements, research, and development;

(b) **Business Information:** business plans, financial information, pricing strategies, customer and user lists, vendor relationships, marketing plans, sales strategies, product roadmaps, investor communications, and strategic plans;

(c) **Content and Data:** video and image datasets, search library metadata, annotation data, benchmark results, user analytics (in aggregate), and content classification systems;

(d) **Personnel Information:** employee compensation, organizational structure, hiring plans, and human resources records; and

(e) **Third-Party Information:** information received from third parties under obligations of confidentiality, including Third-Party AI Service provider technical details, API keys, partner agreements, and customer data.

### 1.2 Exclusions
Proprietary Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Service Provider; (b) was known to the Service Provider before disclosure by the Company, as evidenced by written records; (c) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction on disclosure; or (d) is independently developed by the Service Provider without use of or reference to Proprietary Information, as evidenced by written records.

### 1.3 Non-Disclosure Obligations
The Service Provider agrees to:

(a) Hold all Proprietary Information in strict confidence and not disclose it to any third party without the Company's prior written consent;

(b) Use Proprietary Information solely for the purpose of performing services for the Company;

(c) Take reasonable precautions to prevent unauthorized disclosure, including at minimum the same degree of care the Service Provider uses for their own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care;

(d) Not copy or reproduce Proprietary Information except as necessary for authorized purposes;

(e) Promptly notify the Company of any unauthorized disclosure or use of Proprietary Information.

### 1.4 Duration
The obligations under this Section 1 shall survive termination of the Service Provider's relationship with the Company and shall continue for as long as the Proprietary Information remains confidential, but in no event less than five (5) years from the date of disclosure. For trade secrets, the obligations continue for as long as the information qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law.

## 2. INVENTIONS AND INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 2.1 Prior Inventions
The Service Provider has listed on Exhibit A all inventions, discoveries, improvements, works of authorship, and other intellectual property that: (a) were created by the Service Provider prior to the Service Provider's relationship with the Company; (b) are relevant to the Company's business; and (c) are not assigned to the Company under this Agreement ("Prior Inventions"). If no Prior Inventions are listed on Exhibit A, the Service Provider represents that no such Prior Inventions exist.

### 2.2 Assignment of Inventions
The Service Provider hereby irrevocably assigns to the Company all right, title, and interest in and to any and all inventions, discoveries, improvements, original works of authorship, trade secrets, concepts, ideas, know-how, techniques, processes, algorithms, software, AI/ML models, training methodologies, datasets, benchmarks, and other intellectual property (collectively, "Inventions") that are:

(a) Made, conceived, reduced to practice, or developed by the Service Provider, either alone or jointly with others, during the period of the Service Provider's relationship with the Company; and

(b) Related to the Company's business, actual or anticipated research or development, or made using the Company's equipment, supplies, facilities, or Proprietary Information.

### 2.3 Works Made for Hire
The Service Provider acknowledges that all original works of authorship created within the scope of the Service Provider's engagement are "works made for hire" under the U.S. Copyright Act. To the extent that any such work is not deemed a work made for hire, the Service Provider hereby assigns all copyright in such work to the Company.

### 2.4 Moral Rights Waiver
To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, the Service Provider waives any and all moral rights in and to any Inventions, including rights of attribution, integrity, disclosure, and withdrawal.

### 2.5 Assistance
The Service Provider agrees to assist the Company, at the Company's expense, in obtaining, maintaining, and enforcing intellectual property rights in the Inventions, including executing patent applications, copyright registrations, and all other documents reasonably required. If the Service Provider is unable or unwilling to assist, the Service Provider hereby irrevocably designates and appoints the Company as the Service Provider's attorney-in-fact for this purpose.

### 2.6 Records
The Service Provider agrees to keep and maintain adequate written records of all Inventions in the form of notes, sketches, drawings, or other documentation, which records shall be the property of the Company.

### 2.7 Notice Regarding State Law Protections
**California (Labor Code § 2870):** This Agreement does not require assignment of any invention that the Service Provider develops entirely on their own time without using the Company's equipment, supplies, facilities, or Proprietary Information, unless the invention relates to the Company's business or anticipated research/development.

**Similar statutes exist in Delaware (19 Del. C. § 805), Illinois (765 ILCS 1060/2), Minnesota (Minn. Stat. § 181.78), Washington (RCW 49.44.140), and other states.** The Service Provider should review the applicable statute for their state. This Agreement is to be construed in accordance with such statutes.

## 3. RETURN OF MATERIALS

Upon termination of the Service Provider's relationship with the Company, or at any time upon the Company's request, the Service Provider shall promptly return all materials containing Proprietary Information, including documents, files, records, computer programs, data, equipment, and all copies thereof. The Service Provider shall also permanently delete all Proprietary Information from personal devices, cloud storage, and email accounts, and shall certify in writing that such deletion has been completed.

## 4. NON-SOLICITATION

During the period of the Service Provider's relationship with the Company and for twelve (12) months thereafter, the Service Provider shall not, directly or indirectly:

(a) Solicit, recruit, or encourage any employee or contractor of the Company to leave the Company's service; or

(b) Solicit or induce any customer, vendor, partner, or prospective customer of the Company to terminate or reduce their relationship with the Company.

## 5. NOTIFICATION OF NEW EMPLOYER

During the period of the Service Provider's relationship with the Company and for twelve (12) months thereafter, the Service Provider agrees to notify the Company of any new employment or engagement before commencing such employment or engagement. The Service Provider also agrees to provide a copy of Sections 1, 2, and 4 of this Agreement to any new employer or principal.

## 6. REPRESENTATIONS

The Service Provider represents and warrants that:

(a) The Service Provider's performance of this Agreement does not and will not conflict with any other agreement to which the Service Provider is a party;

(b) The Service Provider will not bring to the Company or use in the performance of services any confidential information or trade secrets of any former employer or other third party without that party's written consent;

(c) The Service Provider will not incorporate any Prior Inventions into any Company work without the Company's prior written consent, and if such incorporation is authorized, the Service Provider hereby grants the Company a non-exclusive, royalty-free, perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide license to use such Prior Inventions.
